Advice for Planting Flowers

Flower Selection •  Flower Care •  Environment Advice

Safety Advice:

Protect Yourself From Sun. Be sure to wear clothing with long sleeves to protect against over exposure to the sun. And wear a hat to help stay cool.

Protect Yourself From Cuts. Wear gloves to help protect your hands when working with tools or pulling weeds.

Flower Selection Advice:

Don't Import Plants from Overseas. If you see a plant that looks pretty in an overseas website, check with an expert before ordering and planting the plant in your garden. It might turn out to be one of those invasive species that spreads, and then everyone in your neighborhood will be unfriendly toward you for years.

Match Plants to Sunlight. Read the labels on plant packages and plant them to match their sunlight requirements.

Consider Blooming Times. If you wish to have flowers blooming throughout the warm weather months, make sure the blooming times are spread over all the summer months.

Flower Care:

Water Plants as Needed. If it doesn't rain for a long time, water your plants in the early morning or in the evenings.

Fertilize Appropriately. Check with the experts at the plant store before fertilizing plants. Some fertiilzers might produce a lot of stem growth but inhibit flowering.

Environmental Advice:

Runoff Winds up in the Lakes. Remember that storm drains lead to lakes and rivers, so don't use too much fertilizer or else you will wind up fertilizing the lakes which then stink.
