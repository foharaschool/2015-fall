Places to Visit in Madison Web Page

Modified Week 02

Madison is a friendly mid-western city with mild weather and friendly people. We would like you to travel to Madison for a visit. This Web page lets you know of some nice places to visit in Madison. All of the places mentioned are open to the public free of charge, although donations are appreciated.

Natural Places to Visit

The most noticeable natural feature of Madison is the sparkling blue lakes. Rent a boat, or just lounge on the shore to enjoy them.

For hiking, the UW Arboretum is easily accessible on the near west side. Stop at the visitor center for maps and information.

Another excellent spot for hiking is a peninsula in Lake Mendota, named Picnic Point.

Person-Made Places to Visit

The most noticeable person-made feature of Madison is the Capitol building on the Square. Guided tours are available during the day. Also, visit the Dome Platform to walk outside around the base of the dome for a great view of Madison.

Also near the Square is the Frank L Wright convention center, built on the edge of Lake Monona.

Another location on the Square is the Wisconsin Historical Society museum, full of Wisconsin history.
